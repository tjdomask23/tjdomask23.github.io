A bit more

I'm a UW-Madison CS grad working at Rockwell Automation, in the Milwaukee area. My role now is digital adoption — I build the in-application guidance that makes enterprise software people actually use, and work with process owners across the business to fix where that adoption breaks down. I came into it from computer science and business-systems analysis, where I spent years turning business requirements into working systems. Same problem, flipped: the software already exists — the real question is whether anyone can navigate it.

The throughline in everything I do is systems design. At work it's adoption content and the workflows behind it; before that it was Jira standards and process waste. On my own time it's a fitness app built on actual research, or a tabletop RPG ruleset I designed from nothing and run for five friends. Same instinct in both: build something structured enough to be reliable and flexible enough that people enjoy using it.

Away from a keyboard I'm a competitive-swimmer-turned-rec-leaguer — volleyball and softball these days. I keep a music library that's gotten genuinely out of hand, watch more anime than I'll admit in a professional bio, and spend real time on the worldbuilding side of tabletop games. And I'm a dog person, no qualifiers.

If any of that lines up with something you're working on — or you just want to compare campaign notes — reach out.
