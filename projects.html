FORMA — research-backed fitness platform

A web app that generates personalized body-recomposition plans from a detailed intake — not the one-size-fits-all template most fitness apps ship. A 114-question questionnaire feeds a plan generator that produces meal planning, weekly scheduling, and grocery budgeting, with every recommendation tied back to actual research rather than gym-bro folklore. That citation layer is the whole differentiator.

I'm building the full stack: Next.js front end, Supabase for data and auth, the Anthropic API driving plan generation, and Stripe wired in for when it's ready to charge. It started as a plan I built for myself and turned into the product I wished existed.

Homebrew tabletop RPG system

A complete from-scratch RPG ruleset I designed and run for a group of five — most recently a campaign set in the Star Wars: The High Republic era (232 BBY). "Homebrew" undersells it: custom classes and subclasses, playable species, a weapons-and-crafting system, tiered armor, and a character-flaw mechanic where personality actually changes how the dice behave.

It scratches the same itch as my day job, honestly — designing a system of rules that's balanced enough to be fair and flexible enough that real people enjoy using it. The feedback loop is just a lot more fun: you find out at the table, immediately, what's broken.
